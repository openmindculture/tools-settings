# Apple App Store, TestFlight, Virtual Mac for App Development

## Apple Web Store Developer Verification

Investing $99 from a marketing or career-development budget is highly worthwhile to showcase mobile development proficiency.

Having a live app on the App Store is considered the ultimate validation of your skills by hiring managers and tech recruiters. It proves you understand the entire lifecycle of software engineering: you did not just write a local script, you navigated code signing, continuous deployment, asset management, and a rigorous review process. 

You should wait until you are about 2 to 4 weeks away from being ready to test or release your app.

You should start from scratch and create a brand-new Apple ID using a professional business email address. Reusing your personal iCloud ID will not give you any extra "trust" points with Apple's review team. Apple will additionally check your person by scanning an official ID card. No Prorating: The $99 fee gives you exactly 12 months of access. If you buy it months before your app is finished, you waste paid time while writing code.

### Do You Get an "Official Profile Page"?

No, Apple does not provide a public, social-network-style developer profile page.

### TestFlight beta

Apple web store: developer account costs $99/year, however there is TestFlight Public Link: You can use a free Apple account to run a TestFlight beta. You can share a public link for up to 10,000 testers. However, the builds expire after 90 days, requiring you to upload updates constantly.

If you want your app listed on the official App Store, you still need a paid $99/year Apple Developer Account to sign the code and submit it. Apple does not offer a free tier for individual developers distributing open-source software on the main App Store.

You cannot use TestFlight with a free Apple Developer account.

Because TestFlight uses Apple’s secure cloud infrastructure and undergoes a lightweight App Review process, Apple restricts its use strictly to paid Apple Developer Program members ($99/year).

### Xcode

However, you can still test your open-source app on your physical iPhone completely for free using Xcode.

If you use frameworks like Flutter, React Native, or Godot on Linux Mint, you can write 100% of your code there. However, you will still need a macOS environment at the very end to compile that code into an iOS .ipa file.

### Virtual Mac Online

You can rent a virtual Mac over the internet to access Xcode. You log in from Linux Mint using a Remote Desktop tool. Check Pay-As-You-Go Plans (Best for quick submissions).

### Outdated Legacy Hardware

Up-to-date devices (or online service replacments) matter immensely. If your MacBook is running macOS Sierra or High Sierra, you cannot use it to publish an app to the official App Store, nor can you use it to test on modern iPhones.

### App Removal for unpaid Accounts (except for Sponsored FOSS)

Yes, your app will be removed from the App Store if you do not pay. Apple makes no exceptions for free or open-source software.

The Non-Profit Loophole: Organizations like the Mozilla Foundation (Firefox), the VLC/VideoLAN organization, or Signal Messenger register as official non-profit organizations. This status allows them to successfully qualify for the official Apple Developer Program Fee Waiver, meaning they pay $0 to stay on the store.
